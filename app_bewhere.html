BeWhere Mobile App

Help create a safer and inclusive cities for everyone

Overview

The app was an idea to help raise awareness of sexual harassment/violence in public space, mainly targeting women. After studying the case together as a group, this app would allow further study into different locations collecting data from ratings, reports and status updates. Using the phone's GPS location, the app will automatically ask you to rate the current location first before using the app itself. Users can see the status update of recent incidents from friends as well as anonymous depending on your set area radius.


Objective

The objective of this project is to present a technological intervention to solve problems within the harrassment of women in public space.



Background Research

Public urban space should generally be accessible to all types of people. The experience of public space can be shaped by the dimensions of one’s identity, racism, sexism, ableism, homophobia and ageism which are often excluded in public space. In the 21st century, there is a rapid urbanisation taking place throughout the world where safety has become the main concern especially for women. Across the news and media, violence against women has become a part of everyday life. Limitations in women participating a social environment can derived from the fear of sexual harassment when simply walking on the city streets, selling their goods or commuting on public transports. This impact their right to freedom movement which can ultimately lead to poor opportunity for work and education suffer.

According to the UN Women research, 92% of women had experienced sexual harassment in their lifetime (UN Women), 30% of Australian girls and young women ages 15-19 in a survey commissioned by Plan International and Our Watch agreed that “girls should not be in public places after dark”.


Problem Statement

How might we raise awareness of sexual harrassment to women in a public space?

Prototype

The beware app is targeted for women or anyone gender feeling unsafe in a public space. After signing up and creating a profile, the user can rate the location, report, explore map, dashboard, status update of other people.

Status newsfeed

The status newsfeed features allow the user to view the recent updates of what the people within their friend's list (or anonymous if within the selected radius) when confronted a harrassment or felt uncomfortable in a public space.

Dashboard view

The user may switch to the Dashboard view for a more analytical overview of the location the user is in. Otherwise, the user can also change the location from the dropdown to also view the statistics for other location.

Explore map

Explore map allows the user to view multiple posts within the selected radius from the base location. This feature can help raise awareness of the surroundings and help the government analyse the harrassment that occurs in different place.

report/witness incident

This tool is for reporting an incident when the user feels unsafe or has experienced harrassment within the place. If witnessed an incident, the user can help raise awareness to others by reporting within the app as well.

Rate location

Rate location collects data within that location for further analyse for government use in order to curate a solution to combat harrassment in for location.

Benefits

This app can be seen beneficial for government use and for Built Environment industries. The security department can then take these data and predict the risk and assess the impact of the place in order to project the people. The data is a channel to provide a way harrassment can be minimise in the public space. Urban designers can then take these data to upgrade and renovate the urban space.

Not only for government benefits but also for women out there who are constantly feeling unsafe when out in a public space. As the definition of the public space is designed to be inclusive for everyone, women or any other gender and race, shouldn't feel obligned to use the space due to the feeling of unsafe. This app allows the users to help raise awareness of the surroundings but also a decision making platform when planning to travel to anywhere.

Improvement

If I had more time working on this app, I would start with a user research and interview to understand what it really is like to be in the shoes of those people who are experiencing harrassment in public space.

This app does not have an immediate action to stop the harrassment in public space but also provides an online tool to help raise awareness. There are many types of harrassment and this app certaintly does not solve for ever type of harrassment.

Business model is also another factor if this app was to be taken further into development. Currently there is no business model.
